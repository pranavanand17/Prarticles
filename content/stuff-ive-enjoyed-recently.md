## Creepy Nuts
Creepy Nuts is a japanese band comprising of the rapper R-Shitei & DJ- DJ Matsunaga. Together they've managed to make some of the coolest japanese songs I've ever heard. They've done a lot of anime OPs as well, some of the first few songs I've heard of theirs was Bling-Bang-Bang-Born (from Mashle) & Otonoke (from Dandadan)- but I think some of their best songs are their more unknown ones. Some of my favourites are- Livelihood, To us former prodigies, Noboshiro, Biliken, Chxxai & Doppleganger. Not only do the songs sound cool but songs like- 'To us former prodigies' & 'Noboshiro' have great underlying messages which really resonated with me & I'd reccomend turning on the english captions and reading along to what R-Shitei is saying in these tracks. There's this channel called First Take on YouTube who's whole agenda is to get japanese artists and make them peroform any one of their trakcs but giving them only one take to do so which means they can't mess up & Creepy Nuts' First Take performances are some of my favorites & I'd like more people to check them out.

## Far Cry 3
I don't play much games which means whenever I do it is always a unique and fun experience- this means that games that have come out more than 10 years ago are still enjoyable to me. But my open-mindedness to games wasn't even really required for a game like Far Cry 3 because that is how genuinly good it is & I think it is still a good game even by today's standards. Despite never having played any of the games in the Far Cry universe before I had heard multiple times from multiple different people about the one of the endings of Far Cry 3 & it seemed pretty cool.My friend further sold me on the game by telling me it had one of the best villains in gaming history- Vaas Montenegro & now having played the game I don't know if I agree with the "best villain" part but he certainly is one of the coolest characters I've seen in media.Vaas has very little screen time in the game- only appearing in 3-4 cutscenes before the climax of the game but whenever he does appear he steals the show. Each Vaas cutscene is a really fun watch, watching his little mannerisms, the way he delivers his dialogue is all incredibly captivating, this being most obvious in the infamous 'defenition of insanity' cutscene. Other than Vaas itself the gameplay was fantastic & each mission felt fun & unique, my personal favourite being the weed burning one because it introduced me to the masterpiece that is 'Make it Bun Dem'.

I go about most of my days worrying about other real problems, I go about some other days with a spring in my step, swinging my arms around without a care in the world and I go about a few of my days pondering about the most insignificant things in the most excessive detail. This is a small anecdote on my silent partner in my day to day life, I don't know he's there, I forget he's there but...he is always...there.

## The Sleeper Agent
I think the concept of a sleeper agent is very cool and thinking about insecurity like that somehow makes it a lot cooler. You completely forget it's there and suddenly a certain phrase or a thought will activate it and BOOM! it's in your head again...pretty cool stuff actually.

I wouldn't say I've struggled with insecurity but it has definetly played a huge role in my life...and maybe writing this is a way for me to see if other people relate with me... my assumption being that they do. My first encounter with being insecure was when back in 10th grade I had asked a friend over Instagram how I could remove this very insignificant blemish on my nose to which he said- apply Apple cider vinegar, which I did...
Promptly tearing the skin off my nose and leaving me with an even bigger dark spot that has remained there for the last 6 years which taught me one of two things- 
1. Don't try to play god and
2. don't apply acid to your fucking face. 

I used the cover of covid to wear a mask and cover this for the next few years and even to this day I am bothered by it every now and again.

My second encounter with insecurity is when I was going about my day minding my own business when one of my close friends told something along the lines of, "Blah blah blah blah your nose is big blah blah blah". This remark which was to him just a passing statement proceeded to severly warp my view of reality. I had gone 16 whole years at that point not second guessing the size of my nose to suddenly analyzing it's shape, size & how it got to be the way it was. I would laser focus on other people's noses trying to figure out what separated mine from their's, I would sometimes apply this slightpressure on it unconciously like somehow that would make it smaller. I have since undergone all the 7 stages of grief from denial to acceptance multiple times concerning the subject but every now and then it does still rub me the wrong way.

I'm not really affected by these two much nowadays, but a certain photo angled a certain way or maybe a comment from someone can sometimes shatter through wall Maria, Rose & Sina in my mind and completely shatter any semblence of my self-confidence.

It's been a while since I've written one of these... why am I doing it now?  mainly two reasons:

1. I've been wanting to journal for a while (by while I mean since yesterday night)
2. I saw one of my friends started a blog of his own (check it out here-> https://variable5ive.github.io/thetimemachine/)

both reasons combined I thought to myself: 
> Hey I got one of those blogs myself and fuck it- it's basically public journalling.

So here I am. For context I'm nearing the tail end of my 3rd year summer vacation- that is the summer vacation between the 3rd and 4th year of my college life. So I thought I'd talk about just that and how summer vacation is just a beautiful time where responsibilites melt away & the sun shines on lazy days where an expanse of opportunity, fun & enjoyment is the only thing days are filled with and how ALL OF THAT IS A FUCKING LIE.

## How it's been
To give context to this summer vacation & the phenomenon I will be discussing and am now dubbing- "Summer Reflection", I must first discuss the last two summer vacations I've had & all the previous summer vacations I've had as a child.

Summer vacation used to be the most beautiful thing ever, it used to be everything I described it as- lazy days, endless fun, friends, travelling, grandparents. It was the whole shebang.


I remember distinctly one summer in around 6th grade or something coming back home on the last day of school knowing all responsibilities were out the door, sitting down on my bed downloading a random ass hero fighting game (I've never played since nor do I remember the name of) and sitting down knowingfully well I can just burn the next six hours until dinner time and no one can stop me...

Ah bliss :)

Only for the doorbell to ring, my mom to tell me it's my friends & I should go play with them and me proceeding to throw a fit because she just threw a monkey wrench in all my plans- "FRIENDS BEGONE MUM, I HAVE APEX RAID OF SHADOWS TO PLAY". I then proceeded to cry in front of my mom and my friends for such a pointless reason much to the concern of my mom & the enjoyment of my friends. 

But that was 6th grade, fast forward to the first college summer vacation & to be completely honest I don't remember a thing about it...

I know I had this internship thing for one of my friends dad's companies & I also know I went to my hometown & Bali that year & both of those experiences were great but overall I don't remember much.

All I can remember is wanting to go back to college and wanting to get lost in all it's BS again (Oh, I also beat Arkham Asylum that summer and it was epic).

The next summer was the one that got me kind of fucked up however, what messed me up that summer was me finally internalizing the advent of AI and how it was going to shape my life and everyone else's. I don't want to go too much into it because I've already written another one of these posts about all that got me fucked up about it.

Since then I don't know how to entirely feel on the matter but realize I don't have time to waste worrying about it & that is where I vaguely stand on the matter.

Regardless I went into a whole existential crisis that summer & because these crisis' fuck me up in more ways than one and because I had another life defining one way back in 2021 (wrote bout this too) that still haunts me to this day I was shook knowing that another one had crept up in my life again... that too during my precious summer vacation.


## How it's gone

I started seeing summer vacations differently, realizing that gone were the days of endless joy and fun but now it's become a time for self introspection and pondering even if I don't want it to (hence why I'm calling it "Summer Reflection").

Because college is so taxing and mentally draining, shit is going on all the time and there is constant noise you don't really get that much time to stop and think...which is honestly pretty cool and I really like college & it's GO!GO!GO! energy, but what this means is the second you come home and are ready to chill for the summer, little do you know your mind has secretly been waiting to think bout all the shit it couldn't think about because of all the college mayhem, which may sound great but once again-> monkey wrench in all my plans.

With that being said this summer vacation has been mostly good, I've been keeping myself busy and I'm more or less alright. Me and my family went to Bhutan and it was one of the best trips I've taken in my life so far, I've been actively trying to exercise as well & trying to take that seriously for the first time in...ever (I've never taken it seriously before). I started off making a bunch of YouTube videos and that kept me occupied for a while, met up with friends a lot, went to my hometown again and a lot of other cool stuff. No real existential crisis this time.

The only thing in my mind really is the thought of making sure something like that doesn't happen again- kinda like a DON'T FUCK THIS UP, sorta thing- which can be annoying in-&-in of itself but I've made it work for me.


## How it's going

Right now I've been doing fine more or less, The things I'm keeping myself busy with are the afformentioned exercise- trying to be consistent about it, I've been studying Quantum Computing for this college credit thing & I've been trying to get into Dragon Ball.

I'm currently watching Dragon Ball Z Kai and I've really enjoyed it so far, everything about the show screams (quite literally) THIS IS FUCKIN EPIC. It's also the perfect stream of motivation for anyone trying to get into going to the gym regularly. I'm out here trying to go Super Saiyan in my room all alone, shit's nuts. 

That's bout it, I've got maybe a few more weeks of this and I hope it ends just as nicely as it's gone, sort of a rocky start but more or less smooth sailing in slightly gloomy weather is how I would describe it. Here's to the future!! 🥂

When I was in school I would hear other kids say how much they hated school or how the education system sucks or general talk about how they wished they were anywhere but here at this moment in time. Personally, I didn’t really care for school and if I had a choice I too would have opted not to be in school. I wished I was at home playing a game, watching a TV show, watching the new PewDiePie video. I’d pray for the weekends to come and wish they wouldn’t end so fast when they did. I’d relish the days there was a strike or something, and say unfortunately some important person died and it was a national or a state holiday, regardless of the depressing reason for the day off, I was the happiest person on the planet on those days. So it’s safe to say I went to sleep some nights wishing what it would be like to not attend school, a world where I don’t have to deal with it, when everyday was Sunday, a world where I had no responsibilities and could do whatever I so pleased.I was sure it would be magical.

And that’s where I was wrong.

## End of a decade

My 9th grade was coming to an end and there was this general buzz about this new virus that was spreading from China. Me and my friends would discuss what it would be like if it spread worldwide, would we have to wear masks all the time, could we leave our houses, would we die but most importantly…would schools close?

Sure enough 9th grade finishes up, 10th grade starts up and bada-bing-bada-boom we have a worldwide pandemic on our hands, everyone is quarantined, and schools are closed down indefinitely and teaching is now online.

I couldn’t believe this, it was almost as if god had smiled down on me and every student on this planet and said, “Here you go, enjoy”. I was the most thankful I’ve ever been on that day, “God, It’s a pleasure doing business with you”.

The days went by smoother than ever, I’d join my “online classes”, leave the audio, open Chrome, open YouTube and watch whatever I wanted for however long I wanted. This went on for days on end, me and my friends even made a YouTube channel at one point and had some fun with that, the possibilities were endless. The exams were a piece of cake — and no I’m not going to expose my methods, I’m not stupid, but if you know, you know.

This went on for a long time, one month turned into two & three and I was having the time of my life but something felt wrong…something told me I can’t keep living like this, this isn’t right…I can’t just watch YouTube all day.

I should watch anime all day.

So that’s what I did for the next year…all I did was watch anime for 6 hours a day, I’d wake up, barely get out of bed, I’d turn on my computer and watch anime. I watched it all- JoJo’s Bizzare Adventure, One Piece, Death Note, The Melancholy Of Haruhi Suzumiya, Bakemonogatari. I was consuming anime as if I was starving and it was a food substitute or something. Actually some days, I’d be so lost in my anime I would forget to eat altogether. Life was great though, it was getting a bit repetitive but it was still great, no school, no responsibilities, just endless consumption. I could do this all day. What could go wrong?

Fast forward a year, our final board exams get cancelled as well and now we’re in the11th grade. A whole school year, I spent at home, a dream come true. Only now that dream had been going on for so long it just didn’t feel all that special anymore, it just became my new reality. It didn’t matter if I was in a new grade, I didn’t see any new faces, there was no new gossip to hear, no new friends to make. Just me awake at home again, staring at my computer screen and my computer screen staring back at me. Watching anime became something I was doing on auto-pilot, I didn’t even want to watch another episode but what else could I do, it was habitual. My life became a sort of slop, consume shows because that’s the only thing I knew how to do. Buy manga because I thought it would somehow fill up that internal void. I’d walk into my room and talk to myself- saying things like “Ah, That new Berserk volume sure does fit on that bookshelf nicely”.

It started off with these mundane things, making every day dull no matter what I did, and then the existentialism set in. You don’t ever not have problems in life, old problems go away so you can start thinking about new ones. It was almost as if my whole life I knew I was going to die one day but now that I had endless time to think about anything whatsoever, my brain finally internalized the gravity of what that meant, and once it did I couldn’t get it out of my head. And being stuck in the same environment, stuck doing the same things, with the same thoughts of death on your mind wasn’t a whole lot of fun. Life went from exciting, to depressing and then…it just got worse.

One day I woke up, wishing I hadn’t. I started watching anime again and as I stared blankly at the screen I said to myself, “I enjoy this”. And a voice in my head said “No, I don’t”. So, I said it again, “No, I enjoy this”, and it said again “No, I don’t”. Okay, this is weird…I think to myself, I go over to get some breakfast and oh look it’s my favorite — Chocos. “No, it isn’t”. What the fuck? I couldn’t shake it. “I wanna buy One Piece”.“No, you don’t”. Okay…I’ll try something factual then- “This is a spoon”. “No, it’s not”. “It’s a spoon!”. “It’s a fork”. “IT’S A GODDAMN SPOON”.

“No…It’s a fork”.

If you thought being stuck at home doing the same things was bad, turns out being stuck in your mind is way worse. I lived with this voice in my head, and it drove me crazy everyday. God knows how I even came up with it. I would think of the opposite to everything just because I could. The worst part about it was…you can’t not think about something. Have you ever tried to not think about something? Now you’re thinking about it. It’s a Catch-22 in your brain. This went on for 6 months, and it was the worst time of my life.

But the days kept on passing, 11th grade was over and schools reopened and I was dying to go back. I went to school on the first day of 12th & in a few weeks I was motivated to do things again, I was excited to go to classes, I was overjoyed to see my friends but most importantly- the voice was gone. “I enjoy this”.

Nothing.

“I enjoy this”.

Nada.

“I ENJOY THIS!!!”.

Silence.

Somehow the noise from the world is what suppressed the noise from building up inside of me, I wished I didn’t have to be alone with my thoughts again and school was perfect for that.

What gets me messed up about this whole thing was that if you had asked me in 2019 if I wished to stay home for 2 years and have the opportunity to do whatever I liked, I would have said yes in a heartbeat. I wished such a day would come. But when it finally did…I wished it never had.

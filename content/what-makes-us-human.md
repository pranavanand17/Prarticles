Asking someone what marks they got, you're friend telling you he was talking to a girl, A comment on a person's appearance, being left out in a group, trying to make someone laugh so as to keep the conversation flowing...all of these are just a lot of social situations but I think there is something riddled in all these situations and much more that taps into the essence of our very being...the essence of being human. 

I remember asking this to my very indifferent roommate around 3 years ago, I'd asked him "What is it in these social situations that would separate someone from being a thing or an entity you have no idea of...to suddenly grounding them as a human?". He barely looked up from his phone and said "dunno.". Despite his cosmic indifference to the groundbreaking knowledge I was gonna bestow on him I told him regardless that I believed it was "Insecurity."

Now I'm sure I was a much edgier person 3 years ago and I am not here to try and pretend like insecurity controls anything and everything in our lives...which is prolly not the case but I do think it plays a huge part in it. You can see it in subtle excahnges to more obvious displays of it. People do the things they do because maybe in some ways they are motivated by some sort of insecurity

I wouldn't say I've struggled with insecurity but it has definetly played a huge role in my life...and maybe writing this is a way for me to see if other people relate with me too, my assumption being that they do. My first encounter with being insecure was when back in 10th grade I had asked a friend over Instagram how I could remove this very insignificantfacial blemish on my nose to which he said- apply Apple cider vinegar, which I did...promptly tearing the skin off my nose and leaving me with an even bigger dark spot that has remained there for the last 6 years which taught me one of two things- don't try to play god & don't apply acid to your fucking face. I used the cover of covid to wear a mask and cover this for the next few years and even to this day I am bothered by it every now and again.

My second encounter with insecurity is when I was going about my day minding my own business when one of my close friends told something along the lines of, "Blah blah blah blah your nose is big blah blah blah". This remark which was to him just a passing statement proceeded to severly warp my view of reality. I had gone 16 whole years at that point not second guessing the size of my nose to suddenly analyzing it's shape, size & how it got to be the way it was. I would laser focus on other people's noses trying to figure out what separated mine from their's, I would sometimes apply this slightpressure on it unconciously like somehow that would make it smaller. I have since undergone all the stages of grief from denial to acceptance multiple times surrounding the subject but every now and then it does still rub me the wrong way.
